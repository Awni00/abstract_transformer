> While there is a comparison with RCA, which is closely related work, there seems to be a lack of information on existing related works or literature. 

We are not aware of prior work that directly incorporates relational information into Transformers. However, interestingly, some studies have shown how Transformers can apparently *implicitly* learn abstractions and relations when they are trained on sufficiently large amounts of data. In particular, Webb, Holyoak and Lu ("Emergent analogical reasoning in large language models", Nat. Human Behav., 2023) show how LLMs (GPT-3) show relational reasoning on versions of Ravens matrix type problems, even when not directly trained on such tasks. Other evidence of this implicit relational reasoning ability is discussed in work such as Dasgupta et al. (2024), Srivastava et al. (2023), Wei et al. (2022), and Chan et al. (2024) and we will add a discussion of this line of work to the revised paper. One implication of our study, however, is that by directly incorporating a relational attention mechanism as we do in *DAT*, it is possible to perform relational tasks with much greater sample efficiency, while also improving on tasks that benefit of relational and sensory information processing, such as language modeling and image classification.

> In Transformers, they also use symbolic tokens (e.g., position embedding). Might it be more precise to add the symbol vector to the context tokens on the right-hand side of Figure 1 (a)? 


This is a good point. Indeed, one type of symbol we consider are relative position encodings, and these are added to the embeddings and are propagated through the 'residual stream' and the values in self-attention. Further, in the use of positional encoding in Rotary Position Embedding or RoPE (Su et al, 2021), relative positional encodings are added in each self-attention head. 

However, the key aspect of relational attention in this work is that the values that are transmitted in message passing do not contain sensory information; the values are relational, and the positional encoding symbols are only used to identify which objects the relations pertain to. We will expand on this point and mention the reviewer's good point about standard positional encoding viewed as symbols in the discussion.

> In the Dual Attention process, sensory and relational embeddings are concatenated. I wonder how they are pooled before entering the next layer. 

This is another good point. One can imagine different types of pooling operations, for example adding the two embeddings in the residual stream. We have chosen to concatenate them, and then they are processed together by the MLP that is part of each layer. Intuitively, the MLP can learn to select from and combine the sensory or relational information as needed. In future work it will be interesting to explore alternative ways of combining (pooling) the different sources of information.
